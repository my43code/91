Mr. Ted Kuaie Alau

Human Resource Manager

Oversees admissions, certification, and digital records to keep learners on track.

Keeping students moving forward

My Name is Mr. Ted Kuaie Alau. I come from Jiwaka Province and currently living in Port Moresby. I like watching football and support the Brisbane Broncos.
My professional experience is Higher Education and Human resources. I like working with young people and spent 40 years working and helping young people to my progress in life. I was educated from grades 1-6 at Tsigmil Primary School run by the Evangelical Brotherhood Church (EBC) outside of Minj in Jiwaka. In 1969 I was sent to Awaba High School in the Western Province to complete grades 7-10 in a school run by the Aisia Pacific Christian school from 1969 to 1972.

In 1973 I completed my Preliminary Year program at the University of Papua New Guinea and was matriculated to do my Bachelor’s degree program in 1974. I majored in Education and graduated with Bachelor of Education degree in 1977. In 1978 to 1979 took a job as graduate assistant within the Registrar’s office at the University of PNG. In 1980 I got transferred to the University of Goroka to work as Assistant Registrar. In 1985 I won an Australian Government scholarship to do my first Masters degree at the University of New England in Australia.

I completed my master’s program in 1986 and returned to work at the University of Goroka. From 1987 I worked as a Senior Assistant Registrar and got promoted to Deputy Registrar in 1989. In 1996 I won a second Australian Government scholarship and studied for my second masters in Development studies and human resources at the Australian National University (ANU) in Canberra, ACT. I successfully completed my second masters in 1978 and returned to UOG.

In 2001 won a position as Deputy Secretary to work for the DHERST and worked for 6 years. As Deputy Secretary, I managed the TESAS scholarship program until my position was made redundant at the end of 2006. In 2007 I was requested by the President of Divine Word University to work in the student services office as an executive Officer. In 2015 I was given the job of Vice President, Student Affairs when my Boss passed away. I worked in that position until the end of 2022 and returned to Jiwaka.
